### Fermat's little theorem

If $p$ is prime, then for any integer $a$, we have $p | ( a^{p} - a )$

or $a^{p-1} \equiv 1 (\mod p)$,  $p$ are prime


Note:

$a^b \mod p \neq a^{b \mod p} \mod p$

### Inverse 
To find $a^{-1} \bmod p$, given $p$ is prime

$p$ must be prime 

$inverse = a^{p-2} \mod p$

$a \cdot x \equiv 1 \mod p$

$x = (a^{p-2}) \mod p$
